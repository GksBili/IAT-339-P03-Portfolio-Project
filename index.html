Project

IAT312 Game Design Project "Vie"

Project Overview

This project is an economic strategy board game developed through an iterative design process. The initial prototype was created in Figma to visualize the board layout, card systems, and gameplay flow before moving into physical prototyping. The game focuses on simulating real-world economic decision-making by allowing players to accumulate, convert, and trade assets while balancing risk, investment, and long-term strategies. Players compete to maximize their Life Score through different paths such as property investment, market trading, and collection strategies.

My Contribution

As a game designer and illustrator, I contributed to the overall game design process, including concept development, physical prototyping, card design, balancing, playtesting, and refinement. I helped redesign card layouts and visual systems to improve readability and distinguish different card categories. I also participated in adjusting gameplay mechanics based on player feedback, including refining Deal Cards, Life Cards, and Market Cards to create clearer interactions and more balanced strategic choices.

Card Design & Visual System

The redesigned card system features improved visual hierarchy with clearer distinctions between different card types and categories.

Challenges & Solutions

During playtesting, we identified several issues including unclear card categories, complicated calculations, and balance problems caused by strong early-game advantages. Some players were confused about the differences between assets and collectibles, while others experienced snowballing effects from powerful cards. To address these issues, we redesigned the card structure with clearer visual hierarchy and descriptions, separated asset-generating cards from collectible items, and adjusted card values and effects based on playtest results. We also introduced more balanced comeback mechanics and simplified market effects to reduce player confusion and improve the overall pacing and fairness of the game.

IAT445 Level Design Project "Out of Barrier"

The Problem/Context

The goal of this project was to design a first-person immersive environment that could be explored and from which stories could be told through the use of the environment. The main problem to be solved was to design a context that draws the player's attention without relying on explicit instructions or a User Interface. Instead, the design of the space and visual framing would be relied upon to influence the actions of the player.

My Role & Process

As a level designer, I had to consider the design of the environment, the paths available to the player, and the design of the player's actions. My first iterations included a highly explorative environment, but this meant too many choices and no focus. To counter this, I added a design element, a narrow opening made of bricks, which creates a focus at the end of the tunnel. This also provided a less explorative environment and meant that interaction from the player was more directed. Through the use of reads and many iterations of the environment I ensured that movement from the player was designed and felt natural.

The Solution & Ethos

My final environment proves that the movement of a player can be designed and focused through the use of space. It also supports my design ethos that a space can be immersive and explorative in nature.
